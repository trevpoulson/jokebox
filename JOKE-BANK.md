# Joke Bank

Full candidate pool, sourced from the web plus already-verified classics. Each
category lists every candidate considered, with a verdict:

- **KEEP** — passes content policy + is actually funny/coherent
- **CUT — content** — fails the "not derogatory toward women/minorities" rule
- **CUT — weak** — doesn't fail content policy, but the logic doesn't hold together or there's no real punchline turn
- **CUT — too far** — crosses from "R-rated risqué" into genuinely non-consensual, involves a minor, or otherwise well past the line regardless of wordplay quality

Rules in force (see the `jokebox-joke-standards` memory): no derogatory content toward any minority group or toward women; ribbing men is fine; mutual husband/wife ribbing is fine as long as neither side is the target of real cruelty; the joke must actually work, not just look joke-shaped; prefer verified/classic jokes over invented ones.

Sources: [300 Best Dad Jokes – TODAY](https://www.today.com/life/dad-jokes-rcna27325), [Dad Jokes – The Company of Dads](https://thecompanyofdads.com/dad-jokes/), [125+ Hilarious Jokes for Kids – Frugal Fun 4 Boys](https://frugalfun4boys.com/hilarious-jokes-for-kids/), [35 Best Dirty Jokes – Inspirationfeed](https://inspirationfeed.com/best-dirty-jokes/), [69 Dirty Jokes – Bored Panda](https://www.boredpanda.com/dirty-jokes/), [105+ Adult Jokes – Humor Living](https://humorliving.com/adult-jokes/)

## Adults Only

This is where most of the filtering happened. Of ~115 candidates pulled from three "top dirty jokes" lists, roughly 40 were cut for content (derogatory toward women, or crossed into non-consent/minors territory) and another ~15 for being incoherent or not actually landing as a joke.

### Cut — content policy (do not use, logged so we don't re-add these later)

- "Guitar teacher arrested for fingering a minor" / "Technology teacher who touches students — a PDF file" — involve child sexual abuse as the punchline. Absolute exclusion regardless of wordplay.
- "How do you get a nun pregnant? Dress her up as a choir boy." — same category of issue (deception enabling abuse), excluded.
- "What do you do when a woman's choking? Back up a few inches." — callous toward a woman's wellbeing during sex, reads as non-consensual disregard.
- "Cinderella at the ball — she gagged." — non-consent-flavored, cut.
- "Sailor tells his date she's tight — wrong hole, plenty of room in the other one" — non-consent-adjacent confusion, cut.
- "Three pregnant women, blonde says can't wait to see my puppies" — "dumb blonde" stereotype, cut.
- "Turn a fox into an elephant — marry her" — body-shaming a wife, cut.
- "Difference between a pregnant woman and a lightbulb — you can unscrew a lightbulb" — implies women trap men / dark undertone, cut.
- "6.9 — another good thing screwed up by a period" — mocks female biology, cut.
- "Two important holes in a woman — her nostrils" — reduces women to body parts, cut.
- "Pick-pocket vs. peeping Tom — one watches your snatch" — same issue, cut.
- "Best thing about gardening — down and dirty with your hoes" — "hoes" as a slur for women, cut.
- "Woman and a road alike — they both have manholes" — cut, same reason.
- "Ugliest kids from which sexual position — ask your mom" — slut-shaming framing, cut.
- Several sex-worker-as-punchline jokes ("hooker and a drug dealer," "hooker with her hand up her skirt," "hooker and a bungee jump") — cut as a blanket policy call, not worth the risk of reading as degrading.
- A couple of jokes using a child character ("Little Johnny") set up next to genital/sex content, and one built around a kid's mishearing of parents having sex — cut; even framed as naive misunderstanding, mixing a child character with sexual content isn't a direction this project should go.
- "Dr. Pepper comes in a bottle — his wife died" — dark, non-sequitur, implied domestic tragedy, cut.
- Two jokes that only worked by using sexual orientation itself as the "gotcha" (a lesbian-themed pun that didn't otherwise make sense on its own) — cut as inconsistent with the "no minority group" rule, even without clear malice.

### Cut — weak/incoherent

- "If a threesome is three people and a twosome is two, why do they call you handsome?" — doesn't parse as an actual joke.
- "Two lesbians in a closet — a liquor cabinet" — fragment, doesn't connect.

- "Break a beer bottle vs. a mirror vs. a condom — condom walks off laughing" — garbled, unclear premise.
- A few others in the same vein, omitted for brevity.
